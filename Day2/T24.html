"Wings of Fire" is an inspiring autobiography of Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, the former President of India and a renowned aerospace scientist. The book provides a deep insight into his early life, education, struggles, and achievements. It portrays how a simple boy from Rameswaram rose to become one of India's most respected scientists and leaders.

I initially assumed the book would focus only on science and technology, making it too technical to enjoy. However, it turned out to be a beautiful blend of personal stories, historical context, and philosophical reflections. The narrative is both humble and powerful, filled with lessons on perseverance, hard work, and faith.

The book also highlights the importance of teamwork and mentorship. Kalam's experiences working with great scientists like Vikram Sarabhai and Satish Dhawan are particularly enlightening. His gratitude toward his family, teachers, and colleagues makes the story feel deeply human and relatable.

Some parts felt overly detailed in terms of scientific explanations, which might not appeal to every reader. But for anyone interested in India's space and defense programs, these insights are invaluable. The book is a tribute to India's scientific development and a call to the youth to dream big and never give up.
